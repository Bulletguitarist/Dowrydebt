The Problem

What the law doesn't capture

Dowry deaths make headlines. The slow financial stranglehold that precedes them does not.

  • Household Debt from Dowry DemandsFamilies take loans, sell land, mortgage homes — debts that follow them for decades. Never recorded as "dowry-related" in any official dataset.
  • Asset Liquidation for MarriageJewellery, livestock, agricultural land — assets built over generations are liquidated. The financial shock destabilises entire households.
  • Long-term Financial CoercionAfter marriage, demands continue. Women are forced to remain in unsafe situations because their family cannot afford the cost of separation.
  • No District-level Data ExistsNCRB records dowry deaths and complaints. There is no database that maps dowry-induced financial distress at a geographic level that policy can act on.
  • Shame and SilenceSocial stigma prevents reporting. Families believe taking loans for a daughter's wedding is normal. The abuse hides inside accepted cultural practice.
